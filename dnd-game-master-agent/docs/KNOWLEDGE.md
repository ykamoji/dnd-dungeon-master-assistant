# Knowledge Index — Tomb of Annihilation

This index maps the *Tomb of Annihilation* D&D 5e adventure module so an agent can locate and load only the markdown files relevant to a query. Read the folder-level descriptions first to narrow down the area of the adventure, then read each file's description to decide which specific files to load.

The adventure follows a worldwide death curse afflicting everyone ever raised from the dead. The party travels to the jungle peninsula of Chult to find and destroy the Soulmonger, progressing from Port Nyanzaru, through the wilderness, into the lost city of Omu, the yuan-ti Fane beneath it, and finally the multi-level Tomb of the Nine Gods.

## [Chapter 2 — The Land of Chult](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/)

The wilderness exploration sandbox: dozens of self-contained keyed locations scattered across the jungle, rivers, bays, valleys, and ruins that the party can discover while traveling overland.

- [The Expedition Begins.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/The%20Expedition%20Begins.md) - Chapter 2 introduction explaining the overland-travel sandbox: possible starting points, the open-ended structure, and DM advice for keeping under-leveled parties alive. Read first for how wilderness exploration works.
- [Aldani Basin.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Aldani%20Basin.md) - A marshy upland basin where the Soshenstar and Tath rivers rise, full of dinosaurs and biting insects. Secret home of the aldani (lobsterfolk) thought long extinct.
- [Ataaz Kahakla.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Ataaz%20Kahakla.md) - The "Gorge of Death," a river canyon whose cliffs are lined with dead but still-colorful coral and embedded skeletons of ancient sea creatures. A scenic but eerie landmark on the River Tath.
- [Ataaz Muhahah.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Ataaz%20Muhahah.md) - The "Laughing Gorge," a chasm crossed by the ancient Monkey Bridge of living monkeys and carved stone ones. Echoes occasionally merge into a haunting, gloating laughter.
- [Ataaz Yklwazi.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Ataaz%20Yklwazi.md) - The "Gorge of Blades," a volcanic canyon studded with razor-sharp basalt blades. A firenewt-patrolled gateway guarding the northern approach to the Valley of Lost Honor.
- [Bay of Chult.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Bay%20of%20Chult.md) - The bay all ships must cross to reach Port Nyanzaru, guarded by Aremag, a one-eyed dragon turtle that extorts treasure tribute. Read for the seaborne arrival/departure encounter.
- [Camp Righteous.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Camp%20Righteous.md) - An abandoned, burned-out riverside camp beneath an 80-foot statue of a man with a crocodile, marking a tunnel entrance. The original of the two crusader camps (contrast Camp Vengeance).
- [Camp Vengeance.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Camp%20Vengeance.md) - A struggling timber fortress of the Order of the Gauntlet, walled and stake-ringed, beset by disease and undead. A potential ally base and quest hub in the jungle.
- [Dungrunglung.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Dungrunglung.md) - The grung realm ruled by despotic King Groak, who has built a mud shrine to summon and mate with the goddess Nangnang. Undead threats make the grungs unusually willing to bargain with outsiders.
- [Firefinger.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Firefinger.md) - A 300-foot rock spire topped by a flaming beacon, its cave-pocked walls hung with frail ladders. A pterafolk nesting site overlooking the dangerous River Tiryki.
- [Fort Beluarian.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Fort%20Beluarian.md) - A Flaming Fist stockade flying the flag of Baldur's Gate, commanded by Liara Portyr, atop a steep hillock. A key civilized outpost and alternative expedition launch point.
- [Heart of Ubtao.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Heart%20of%20Ubtao.md) - A floating chunk of earth shaped like a giant stone heart, dripping red liquid, suspended 200 feet above a swampy forest. A surreal, ominous flying landmark.
- [Hisari.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Hisari.md) - A ruined yuan-ti city, half-swallowed underground, warded so yuan-ti cannot enter. Most of its serpent folk have since relocated to Omu.
- [Hrakhamar.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Hrakhamar.md) - A dwarven forge abandoned to volcanic eruptions and now occupied by firenewts crafting arms. Watched from afar by a small band of albino dwarves hoping to reclaim it.
- [Hvalspyd.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Hvalspyd.md) - A massive frost-giant longship with white-dragon-wing sails, anchored off the northeast coast. Crewed by giants led by Drufi, all hunting Artus Cimber and the Ring of Winter.
- [Ishau.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Ishau.md) - A drowned coastal village whose stone buildings now sit underwater in Refuge Bay. Prowled by sharks, plesiosaurs, and sea hags scavenging the ruins.
- [Jahaka Anchorage.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Jahaka%20Anchorage.md) - A hidden pirate haven sheltering three ships (Dragonfang, Emerald Eye, Stirge), only one docked at a time. The base of the pirates of Chult's coast.
- [Jahaka Bay.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Jahaka%20Bay.md) - A wide, mist-shrouded bay fed by the River Tath's waterfalls, with visibility low enough to conceal the pirate anchorage within. The maritime approach to Jahaka Anchorage.
- [Kir Sabal.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Kir%20Sabal.md) - A cliffside aarakocra monastery perched 500+ feet up a plateau, reached by stone steps and rickety walkways. Home to elder Asharra and a refuge tied to the Mbala and Nangalore storylines.
- [Kitcher's Inlet.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Kitcher's%20Inlet.md) - A shallow bay where the River Olung meets the sea, named after a bumbling Cormyrean explorer. Otherwise unremarkable except as access to Port Castigliar and Mezro.
- [Lake Luo.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Lake%20Luo.md) - A geothermal lake heated to boiling by volcanic vents and lava, ringed by dead ash-and-salt wasteland. Hostile to all life but mud and steam mephits.
- [Land of Ash and Smoke.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Land%20of%20Ash%20and%20Smoke.md) - A hellish volcanic sea of black rock veined with lava, far hotter and drier than the rest of Chult. Domain of firenewts and the red dragon Tzindelor, largely unexplored.
- [Mbala.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Mbala.md) - An 1,800-foot, skull-strewn plateau whose villagers were devoured by Nanny Pu'pu, a green hag posing as a witch doctor, guarded by a hidden flesh golem and flying monkeys. She offers the Rite of Stolen Life — a ritual that turns dead PCs into undead — in exchange for clearing the nearby pterafolk nest. Read for the hag encounter, raising the dead via the rite, or the pterafolk quest.
- [Mezro.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Mezro.md) - A Chultan city thought destroyed by the Spellplague but secretly transported to a demiplane by its immortal barae defenders, leaving decoy ruins. Tied to Artus Cimber, whose wife Alisanda is one of the barae.
- [Mistcliff.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Mistcliff.md) - A 1,000-foot, 200-mile wall of volcanic rock along the west coast with no beach, only jagged rocks and crashing waves. Home to large aarakocra settlements and flying-reptile nests.
- [Nangalore.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Nangalore.md) - A ruined tiered garden built for the vain Omuan queen Zalkoré, who became a medusa through an erinyes's bargain. Now the "Garden of Lost Dreams," haunted by her presence.
- [Needle's Bones.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Needle's%20Bones.md) - A wide, frog-filled sinkhole littered with goblin bones, its murky green water 30 feet down. A grim natural hazard in the jungle.
- [Nsi Wastes.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Nsi%20Wastes.md) - A blighted, poisonous wasteland holding the ruined palace of Ras Nsi, once borne on giant undead turtles. Random-encounter frequency is doubled here.
- [Omu.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Omu.md) - A brief stub pointing to the full treatment of the lost city of Omu in chapter 3. Read the chapter 3 files instead for actual Omu content.
- [Orolunga.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Orolunga.md) - A crumbling ziggurat guarded by a millennia-old guardian naga who is perhaps the best source of lore in Chult. A key destination for information-seeking parties.
- [Port Castigliar.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Port%20Castigliar.md) - A "port" that is really an abandoned beach with a derelict supply depot, a few huts, and a defiled, ghoul-dug graveyard. Driven empty by undead.
- [Refuge Bay.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Refuge%20Bay.md) - A relatively safe white-sand landing spot favored for starting expeditions, near the sunken town of Ishau. A common alternative entry point into Chult.
- [River Olung.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/River%20Olung.md) - A river flowing from Lake Luo that guides recognize as the boundary between "normal" jungle to its east and undead territory to its west. Read for navigation and the undead frontier.
- [River Soshenstar.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/River%20Soshenstar.md) - A major river running north from the Aldani Basin to the Bay of Chult, broken by waterfalls every ten to fifteen miles. A primary canoe route into the interior.
- [River Tath.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/River%20Tath.md) - A river rising in the Aldani Basin and winding west through Ataaz Kahakla into Jahaka Bay. A connector route across the southern jungle.
- [River Tiryki.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/River%20Tiryki.md) - A treacherous river of frequent waterfalls and rapids, lined by predators, undead, pterafolk, and grungs. Considered the most dangerous river in Chult.
- [Shilku Bay.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Shilku%20Bay.md) - A bay poisoned by lava flowing from the volcano that destroyed Shilku, with falling hot stones and ash hazarding ships. Safe landings exist only on the western shore.
- [Shilku.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Shilku.md) - An ash-buried coastal village destroyed and preserved by eruption, eyed by Liara Portyr as the next excavation target. Now infested with firenewts, salamanders, and mephits.
- [Snapping Turtle Bay.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Snapping%20Turtle%20Bay.md) - A strikingly beautiful but hazard-laced bay of giant snapping turtles and dangerous flora, where wary but bribable cyclopes lair. Cyclopes can become valuable guides or bearers.
- [Snout of Omgar.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Snout%20of%20Omgar.md) - A former peninsula split into a large island by the Spellplague, crossed by a narrow strait. Marks the boundary between Chult and Samarach.
- [The Cauldron.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/The%20Cauldron.md) - A bay where lava pouring into the sea produces endless columns of steam, ash, and smoke. Lifeless waters under floating soot, with ash-choked dead coast.
- [Valley of Dread.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Valley%20of%20Dread.md) - A dense jungle pass between mountain ranges, a common eastern entry to Chult and a stomping ground for dinosaurs. Home to savage lizardfolk kingdoms.
- [Valley of Embers.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Valley%20of%20Embers.md) - A burned-out valley around Lake Luo, scoured by pyroclastic flows, lava, and raining embers from the Peaks of Flame. Choked with ash-covered muck and mephits.
- [Valley of Lost Honor.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Valley%20of%20Lost%20Honor.md) - A lava-buried valley where Ras Nsi's undead wiped out the Eshowe tribe, now claimed by firenewts on giant striders. The red dragon Tzindelor is the one power they respect.
- [Vorn.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Vorn.md) - An 8-foot statue that is actually a deactivated shield guardian, revered as a god by local goblin, grung, and vegepygmy tribes who keep the area a neutral zone. It can be reactivated and controlled by whoever finds its control amulet at Yellyark. Read for the shield guardian construct and its Yellyark amulet link.
- [Wreck of the Narwhal.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Wreck%20of%20the%20Narwhal.md) - A seagoing galleon magically stranded upside-down in the jungle, now shared by a reclusive weretiger (a cousin of merchant prince Wakanga O'tamu) and a vegepygmy tribe. A strange, peaceful hideaway.
- [Wreck of the Star Goddess.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Wreck%20of%20the%20Star%20Goddess.md) - A non-seagoing flying vessel broken into three chunks high in the tree branches. A wreck whose unusual design hints at a crashed skyship.
- [Wyrmheart Mine.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Wyrmheart%20Mine.md) - A former shield-dwarf iron mine seized by the red dragon Tzindelor ("Tinder") and her kobolds. Hew Hackinstone wants to reclaim it and enlists the party's help.
- [Yellyark.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-2-The%20Land%20of%20Chult/Yellyark.md) - The mask-wearing Biting Ant tribe's Batiri goblin village, built on a sprung branch-net so it can be catapulted away from predators. A whimsical, defensible goblin settlement.

## [Chapter 3 — Dwellers of the Forbidden City (Omu)](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/)

The hidden lost city of Omu: a numbered location crawl through cliff-ringed ruins, including the nine trickster-god shrines that each hold a puzzle cube needed to open the Tomb of the Nine Gods.

- [Omu.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/Omu.md) - Chapter 3 introduction and the city's hub file: the Legend of the Nine Trickster Gods (the origin myth pairing each god with its animal form — Obo'laka/zorbo, I'jin/almiraj, Shagambi/kamadan, Nangnang/grung, Papazotl/eblis, Kubazan/froghemoth, Wongo/su-monster, Moa/jaculi, Unkh/flail snail), the puzzle-cube mechanic, and the race against Ras Nsi to collect the nine cubes that open the Tomb. Also covers the six factions inhabiting Omu (yuan-ti, grungs, vegepygmies, kobolds, Red Wizards, tabaxi hunters), navigation hazards (blue mist fever, assassin vines, collapsing floors), and the directory of the 20 numbered city locations. Read first for nine-gods lore, how the cubes work, who lives in Omu, or city navigation.
- [1. City Entrance.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/1.%20City%20Entrance.md) - A ruined guardhouse with arrow slits, a lopsided portcullis, and graffiti hinting at the challenges ahead, watched by Ras Nsi's yuan-ti scouts. The party's gateway into Omu and a likely first encounter. Read for the entrance ambush and the warning graffiti.
- [2. Walled Compound.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/2.%20Walled%20Compound.md) - The Red Wizards' former base, where yuan-ti slaughtered the Thayan expedition, leaving thirty corpses, a bone spirit pole, and scavenging wild dogs. The lone survivor, Orvex Ocrammas, hides nearby and can share information about the Red Wizards and Omu. Read for the Thayan expedition's fate or the NPC Orvex.
- [3. Kubazan's Shrine.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/3.%20Kubazan's%20Shrine.md) - Shrine of the trickster god Kubazan (a froghemoth), holding one of the nine puzzle cubes. A froghemoth lurks in the entry pool, and the cube is won past a locked gate in the Chamber of Bravery.
- [4. Shagambi's Shrine.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/4.%20Shagambi's%20Shrine.md) - Shrine of the trickster god Shagambi (a kamadan), holding a puzzle cube, themed as a gladiatorial arena with an arena gallery, gladiator cells, and a fighting pit. Guarded by two mated kamadans defending their cubs.
- [5. Great Rift.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/5.%20Great%20Rift.md) - A ruin-edged rift overlooking a lava lake, where a vegepygmy tribe ritually sacrifices a captive grung. The party can intervene in the sacrifice.
- [6. Moa's Shrine.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/6.%20Moa's%20Shrine.md) - Shrine of the trickster god Moa (a jaculi), holding a puzzle cube, perched on a rock pinnacle 200 feet above the great rift and reached by bridging a 60-foot gap. Inside are a trapped passage and a Chamber of Deception with hidden secret rooms.
- [7. Fallen Tree.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/7.%20Fallen%20Tree.md) - A wide fallen-trunk bridge over a fast river near a waterfall, where the tabaxi hunter Bag of Nails ambushes fording parties. A combat/encounter site.

Unkh's Shrine.md](Tomb-of-Annihilation/Chapters/Ch-3-Dwellers%20of%20the%20Forbidden%20City/8.%20Unkh's%20Shrine.md) - Shrine of the trickster god Unkh (a flail snail), holding a puzzle cube behind heavy 600-pound doors, with a ruined workshop and a Chamber of Contemplation.
